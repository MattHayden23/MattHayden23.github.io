Accent-Emotion Entanglement in LM-Based TTS Synthesis

Matt Hayden
University of Edinburgh
Master's Dissertation, 2025
Paper Supplementary Code arXiv

Abstract

Many zero-shot TTS systems now claim to produce near-human quality imitations of speech. However, the recent growth in features and the rush to report state-of-the-art results can lead to unexpected issues slipping through the cracks. This paper investigates such an issue, a new phenomenon dubbed accent-emotion entanglement. It is found that, in CosyVoice 2 (du_cosyvoice_2024), an instruction-based zero-shot TTS system, accent hallucination is caused and guided by the emotion of the input provided to the model. A similar zero-shot model, MaskGCT (wang_maskgct_2024), does not produce the same results. The paper also discusses the importance of subjective results in evaluation as a means to avoiding future unexpected issues.
